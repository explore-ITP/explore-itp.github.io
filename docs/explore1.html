Changes in Temperature & Salinity with Depth and through Time

Interactive Plots

The set of visualizations below show changes in temperature and salinity with ocean depth for ITP1, the first ice-tethered profiler. Pressure is closely correlated with depth and thus the terms are interchangeable in this context. Pressure is measured in decibars (dbar), temperature in degrees Celsius (°C), and salinity in practical salinity units (psu).

Scroll through the three interactive plots using the arrow buttons and see their descriptions below.


Changes with Depth

The first visualization demonstrates each profile through the seasons of the machine's journey. Winter is pictured as dark blue, followed by spring in green, summer in pink and fall in light blue. The tabs can be used to change the scope of the plot, either viewing all temperature profiles, all salinity profiles, or profiles by season. Notice how pressure changes on the y-axis. It may seem weird that the numbers become smaller further away from the origin. The top of the graph corresponds to the surface of the ocean, and therefore dbar equals 0 and increases moving towards the origin.

Changes with Time

The second and third plots are heatmaps, which indicate temperature or salinity through the use of color and over a period of time. We can see that the y-axis from the first set of graphs is the same, thus conserving the spatial context. A day’s worth of temperature and salinity data is averaged for each interval of depth, each day becoming a snapshot in time. Therefore, the heatmaps represent consecutive snapshots over the length of an ITP machine’s active lifetime.

Notice that the range of days on the y-axes differ, and some days have less color than others. This is due to the difference in active lifetimes and the variability of how much data is recorded each day. Some ITP machines malfunction, and some batteries deplete faster than others.
