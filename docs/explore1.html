The set of visualizations above show changes in temperature with pressure followed by changes in salinity with pressure. Pressure is closely correlated with depth and thus the terms are interchangeable in this context. Pressure is measured in decibars (dbar), temperature is measured in degrees Celsius (°C), and salinity is measured in practical salinity units (psu). The colors represent intervals of time. Winter: Dark Blue, Spring: Green, Summer: Pink, and Fall: Light Blue. With the same y-axis and color usage, the graphs above are easily comparable.

Notice how pressure changes on the y-axis. It may seem weird that the numbers become smaller further away from the origin. The top of the graph corresponds to the surface of the ocean, and therefore dbar equals 0 and increases moving towards the origin.

Questions for thought:

What changes or patterns do you observe throughout the water column? Between seasons? What range of temperature do you observe? Salinity? How could you interpret the steep drop-off present on every salinity graph, beginning around 400 dbar?

Changes in Temperature & Salinity over Time

Let’s reorient ourselves to understand the visualizations above, known as heatmaps. First, we conserve the y-axis from the first set of graphs, thus conserving the spatial context. The range of x values from above previously denoting temperature or salinity now correspond instead to a range of colors. A day’s worth of temperature and salinity data is averaged for each interval of depth, each day becoming a snapshot in time. The heatmaps represent consecutive snapshots over the length of an ITP machine’s active lifetime, temperature on the left and salinity on the right.

Notice that the range of days on the y-axes differ. This is due to the difference in active lifetimes. Some ITP machines malfunction, and some batteries deplete faster than others.

Questions for thought:

What changes or patterns do you observe over time? Can you match patterns to those observed in the earlier graphs, for example with ITP 49 and salinity? If so, how do you interpret them? What can you infer from the consistency of patterns over time for temperature? For salinity?
